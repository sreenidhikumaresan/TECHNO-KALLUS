ChocoChip CRUMB Recipe

Chocochip Biscuit Crumble Cheesecake
Ingredients:
1 package (15.5 oz) chocolate chip biscuits, crushed
6 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
32 oz cream cheese, softened
1 cup granulated sugar
1/4 cup packed light brown sugar
4 large eggs
1 teaspoon vanilla extract
1/4 cup all-purpose flour
1/2 cup milk chocolate chips
Instructions:
Prepare the crust: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). In a medium bowl, combine the crushed chocolate chip biscuits and melted butter. Press the mixture into the bottom of a 9-inch springform pan. Bake for 10 minutes, then set aside to cool.  
Make the filling: In a large bowl, beat the cream cheese until smooth. Gradually add the granulated sugar and brown sugar, beating until well combined. Beat in the eggs one at a time, then stir in the vanilla extract.
Combine the filling and crust: Pour the filling over the cooled crust. Sprinkle the milk chocolate chips evenly over the top.
Bake: Bake for 50-60 minutes, or until the cheesecake is set around the edges but still slightly jiggly in the center. Turn off the oven and leave the cheesecake inside for an hour to cool gradually.
  Refrigerate: Refrigerate the cheesecake for at least 4 hours, or overnight, before serving.
Optional toppings: Serve with a dollop of whipped cream, a drizzle of chocolate sauce, or fresh berries

COCO Bisq Recipe

Coconut Almond Biscotti Brittle
Ingredients:
1 package (15.5 oz) coconut almond biscotti, crushed
1/2 cup granulated sugar
1/4 cup light corn syrup
1/4 cup unsalted butter, cut into pieces
1/4 teaspoon salt
1/4 cup toasted coconut flakes
Instructions:
Preheat oven: Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
Combine ingredients: In a large saucepan, combine crushed biscotti, sugar, corn syrup, butter, and salt. Bring to a boil over medium heat, stirring constantly.
Cook until thickened: Continue cooking, stirring occasionally, until the mixture reaches 250°F (121°C) on a candy thermometer.
Add coconut flakes: Remove from heat and stir in toasted coconut flakes.
Pour onto baking sheet: Immediately pour the mixture onto the prepared baking sheet and spread into a thin layer.
Cool and break: Let cool completely, then break into pieces.
Optional variations: Add a pinch of cinnamon or nutmeg for a spiced flavor. Drizzle with melted chocolate or sprinkle with sea salt for a sweet and salty contrast.

Cafe Bisq Recipe

Coffee-Infused Chocolate Chip Cookie Ice Cream Sandwiches
Ingredients:
1 package (15.5 oz) coffee-infused chocolate chip biscuits
1 quart vanilla ice cream, slightly softened
1/2 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips
1 tablespoon coconut oil
Instructions:
Prepare the biscuits: Cut coffee-infused chocolate chip biscuits in half horizontally.
Soften ice cream: Let the vanilla ice cream soften slightly.
Assemble sandwiches: Place a scoop of softened ice cream between two biscuit halves. Press together gently.
Freeze: Place assembled sandwiches on a baking sheet and freeze for at least 1 hour, or until firm.
Melt chocolate: Melt chocolate chips and coconut oil together in a double boiler or microwave, stirring until smooth.
Drizzle chocolate: Drizzle melted chocolate over frozen sandwiches.
Freeze again: Freeze for at least 30 minutes, or until chocolate is set.
Serve: Serve immediately or store in an airtight container in the freezer.
Optional variations: Add a sprinkle of espresso powder or a drizzle of caramel sauce for an extra coffee kick.

BisqFills Recipe

Bisqfills Brownie Bites
Ingredients:
1 package (15.5 oz) Bisqfills biscuits, crushed
1/2 cup unsalted butter, melted
1 cup granulated sugar
1/2 cup packed light brown sugar
2 large eggs
1 teaspoon vanilla extract
1/2 cup all-purpose flour
1/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
1/4 teaspoon salt
1/2 cup semi-sweet chocolate chips
Instructions:
Preheat oven: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). Line a muffin tin with paper liners.
Prepare the crust: In a medium bowl, combine the crushed Bisqfills biscuits and melted butter. Press the mixture into the bottom of each muffin cup.
Make the filling: In a large bowl, whisk together the granulated sugar, brown sugar, eggs, and vanilla extract until well combined.
Add dry ingredients: In a separate bowl, whisk together the flour, cocoa powder, and salt. Gradually add the dry ingredients to the wet ingredients, mixing until just combined.  
Stir in chocolate chips: Stir in the semi-sweet chocolate chips.
Pour into muffin cups: Pour the batter evenly into the prepared muffin cups.
Bake: Bake for 15-20 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
Cool: Let cool in the muffin tin for a few minutes before transferring to a wire rack to cool completely.
Optional toppings: Serve with a dollop of whipped cream, a drizzle of chocolate sauce, or a sprinkle of powdered sugar.

Ginger Biscuits Recipe

Ginger Bisq and Chocolate Tart
Ingredients:
1 package Ginger Bisq biscuits, crushed
6 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
1 cup granulated sugar
1/4 cup cocoa powder
1/4 teaspoon salt
1 large egg
1/2 cup heavy cream
1/4 cup dark chocolate chips
Instructions:
Prepare the crust: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). In a medium bowl, combine the crushed Ginger Bisq biscuits and melted butter. Press the mixture into the bottom of a 9-inch tart pan. Bake for 10 minutes, then set aside to cool.
Make the filling: In a large bowl, whisk together the sugar, cocoa powder, and salt. Beat in the egg until combined. Stir in the heavy cream.
Pour into crust: Pour the filling into the cooled crust.
Bake: Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the filling is set.
Top with chocolate chips: Sprinkle the dark chocolate chips over the warm tart.
Cool and serve: Let cool completely before serving.
Optional toppings: Serve with a dollop of whipped cream or a scoop of vanilla ice cream.

Marie biscuits Recipe

Marie Bisq and Cream Cheese Frosting Bars
Ingredients:
1 package Marie Bisq biscuits, crushed
6 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
1 cup granulated sugar
1/4 cup all-purpose flour
1/4 teaspoon salt
1/2 cup cream cheese, softened
1/4 cup powdered sugar
1 teaspoon vanilla extract
1/4 cup milk chocolate chips
Instructions:
Prepare the crust: Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C). In a medium bowl, combine the crushed Marie Bisq biscuits, melted butter, sugar, flour, and salt. Press the mixture into the bottom of a 9x13 inch baking pan. Bake for 10 minutes, then set aside to cool.
Make the frosting: In a small bowl, beat together the cream cheese, powdered sugar, vanilla extract, and milk until smooth.
Spread frosting: Spread the frosting evenly over the cooled crust.
Top with chocolate chips: Sprinkle the milk chocolate chips over the frosting.
Refrigerate: Refrigerate for at least 1 hour, or until the frosting is set.
Cut into bars: Cut into desired size bars and serve.
Optional toppings: Serve with a dollop of whipped cream or a drizzle of chocolate sauce.
